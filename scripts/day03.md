## Part 1. Personal Questions (00:00 - 04:00)

**Q1. Describe a time when you had to overcome a difficult challenge at work.**
> One of the most difficult challenges I faced was when our team was assigned an urgent project with a very tight deadline. At first, I felt overwhelmed because we didn't have enough resources to complete it on time. However, instead of panicking, I organized a quick meeting to redistribute tasks based on each member's strengths. As a result, we managed to submit the project just before the deadline, and the client was satisfied with the quality of our work.

**Q2. What does work-life balance mean to you?**
> To me, work-life balance means being able to fully focus on my job during working hours while still having enough time and energy left for my personal life afterward. It's not necessarily about spending an equal amount of time on both, but rather about making sure that neither one negatively affects the other.

**Q3. How do you handle stress in the workplace?**
> When I feel stressed, I usually try to identify the root cause first rather than just dealing with the symptoms. If the stress comes from having too many tasks, I prioritize them and tackle the most urgent ones first. I also make it a habit to take short breaks throughout the day, since stepping away from my desk can help clear my mind.

## Part 2. Opinion Questions (04:00 - 08:00)

**Q1. Do you think artificial intelligence will replace human jobs in the near future?**
> While I acknowledge that AI will inevitably automate certain repetitive tasks, I don't believe it will completely replace human jobs, at least not in the near future. Many roles require emotional intelligence and complex decision-making, which AI still struggles to replicate. That said, workers will need to continuously update their skills to work alongside AI effectively.

**Q2. Is it more effective for leaders to be strict or flexible with their employees?**
> I believe effective leadership requires a balance between the two, depending on the situation. Being strict can be necessary when it comes to meeting deadlines, since a lack of discipline can lead to inconsistent results. On the other hand, being flexible allows employees to feel trusted, which often leads to higher motivation.

**Q3. Should employees be evaluated mainly based on individual performance or teamwork?**
> In my view, both individual performance and teamwork should be considered, but the weight given to each may vary by role. For positions that require a lot of independent work, individual performance might be more relevant. However, for roles that heavily depend on collaboration, teamwork should be weighted more heavily.
