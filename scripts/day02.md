## Part 1. Personal Questions (00:00 - 04:00)

**Q1. What do you find most rewarding about your current job?**
> Honestly, the most rewarding part of my job is seeing a project succeed after a lot of hard work. For example, last month our team finished a big project ahead of schedule, and it felt great to see everyone's effort pay off. I also enjoy collaborating with colleagues from different departments, because it gives me a chance to learn new perspectives.

**Q2. How do you usually spend your weekends?**
> On weekends, I try to balance rest and self-improvement. For instance, I usually spend Saturday mornings exercising, since it helps me stay energized for the rest of the week. In the afternoon, I often study English or read business articles. On Sundays, however, I prefer to relax completely and spend time with my family.

**Q3. What skill would you like to improve, and why?**
> If I had to choose one skill to improve, it would be public speaking. Although I'm comfortable talking with colleagues one-on-one, I still feel nervous when presenting in front of a large group. I believe that improving this skill would help me communicate my ideas more confidently and advance in my career.

## Part 2. Opinion Questions (04:00 - 08:00)

**Q1. Some people believe experience is more important than education. Do you agree or disagree?**
> I partly agree with this statement. On one hand, experience allows people to apply their knowledge in real situations, which is something a classroom cannot fully teach. On the other hand, education provides a strong theoretical foundation that helps people understand why certain methods work. Therefore, I believe both experience and education are equally important, and one should not be valued over the other.

**Q2. Do you think companies should offer more flexible working hours?**
> Yes, I strongly believe that flexible working hours can benefit both employees and companies. Not only does it allow employees to manage their personal responsibilities more effectively, but it can also increase overall productivity, since people tend to perform better when they can work during their most energetic hours. However, companies should still set clear guidelines to ensure that teamwork is not affected.

**Q3. Should companies prioritize innovation over stability?**
> In my opinion, it depends on the industry. For companies in fast-changing markets, such as technology, innovation is essential for survival. Meanwhile, industries like manufacturing may benefit more from stability, since consistency builds customer trust. Overall, I think a balance between the two is the most effective approach.

> This graph compares the sales performance of Product A and Product B between 2020 and 2024. At the beginning of the period, Product A was outselling Product B by a significant margin. However, starting in 2022, the trend reversed, and Product B's sales grew rapidly, eventually surpassing Product A in 2024. This shift suggests that customer preferences may have changed, possibly due to Product B's improved features or more effective marketing. If this trend continues, Product B is likely to dominate the market in the coming years.

## Part 5. Summarize an Article (16:00 - 20:00)

**[화면 안내: 아래 지문을 화면에 표시]**

> As artificial intelligence becomes more common in the workplace, companies are facing both opportunities and challenges. On one hand, AI can automate repetitive tasks, allowing employees to focus on more creative and strategic work. Several studies suggest that this shift could increase overall productivity by up to 30 percent within the next decade. On the other hand, many workers worry that AI will eventually replace their jobs, leading to increased anxiety in the workplace. To address these concerns, experts recommend that companies invest in retraining programs, helping employees develop skills that complement AI rather than compete with it. Ultimately, how successfully a company manages this transition may determine its long-term competitiveness.

**모범 요약:**
> This article discusses the impact of AI on the workplace. While AI can automate repetitive tasks and potentially boost productivity by up to 30 percent, many employees fear it might replace their jobs. As a result, experts suggest that companies should invest in retraining programs so that workers can develop skills that complement, rather than compete with, AI. Ultimately, the article concludes that how well a company manages this transition could determine its future competitiveness.
